In 2026, developer productivity is no longer measured solely by lines of code, but by the velocity of the "inner loop"
and the seamlessness of the path to production. As we move into the era of agentic AI and highly distributed hybrid
clouds, the "Developer Journey" has faced new frictions from configuration drift to cognitive overload.
This session provides a high level strategic overview of the modern developer journey with following capabilities:

- Augmented Inner Loop: Leveraging AI agents and open-source standards to accelerate local development.
- Standard First Architecture: Why relying on Jakarta EE and MicroProfile creates portable, future-proof systems.
- Observability by Design: Moving beyond simple logging to a standardized telemetry approach that provides instant
  feedback.
- The Path to Production: Eliminating the "Day 2" bottlenecks through automated guardrails and cloud native adoption.
  Whether you are an architect looking to standardize your organization’s output or a developer seeking to reclaim your
  flow state, you will walk away with a blueprint for modernizing your engineering practices and a clear understanding
  of where the developer journey is headed next.

- Key Takeaways:

- Productivity Frameworks: How to identify and eliminate "toil" in your specific development lifecycle.
- Architectural Guardrails: Best practices for balancing developer autonomy with system-level stability.
- Future-Proofing: A look at how Java technologies (Quarkus, Liberty) are reshaping the role of the developer.
